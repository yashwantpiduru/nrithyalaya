About Nrithyalaya

Nrithyalaya Dance Temple is a Bharatanatyam school based in Sydney. We preserve the classical grammar while making the art accessible to modern learners. Training blends adavus, repertoire, rhythm & theory with a strong emphasis on nritta precision and abhinaya expression.

Teaching Approach

  • Structured progression with clear milestones and assessments
  • Foundation to margam repertoire (Alarippu to Tillana)
  • Music & rhythm work — tala, solkattu, and stagecraft
  • Performance opportunities at community events and festivals

Highlights

  • Performances: Mysuru Palace, Jaganmohan Palace, Nrithya Sabha, Dasara Meet, and Sydney showcases
  • Awarded Dance of Honour by Kanika Parameshwari Trust
  • Inclusive classes from age 2 to seniors

Teachers

Guru Sindhu Manognya

Artist • Vidwat‑certified • Bharatanatyam (Pandanallur & Mezhathoor)

Sindhu began learning Bharatanatyam at age 5 under Guru Parvati Vidhyanathan, completing 15+ years of rigorous study before moving into full‑time teaching. Over the past decade in Sydney, she has trained students from ages 2–60, choreographed for festivals, and prepared seniors for arangetram. Her teaching balances classical grammar with individual expression.

  • Styles: Pandanallur, Mezhathoor (Bharatanatyam)
  • Focus: Adavus, Abhinaya, Repertoire, Stagecraft, Theory
  • Notable venues: Mysuru Palace, Jaganmohan Palace, Nrithya Sabha, Dasara Meet; Sydney community festivals
  • Award: Dance of Honour — Kanika Parameshwari Trust
